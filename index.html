Verified in OH's own documents

Three findings that shape everything

1

First-contact date is sanctioned — one direction only

The DSP FAQ: if an attached patient's rostering date is unrecorded, use the date of first contact. If the patient is not attached, use 999999. A date-recovery rule, never an attachment-creation rule.

2

The status field is about our organization only

Spec wording: report whether the patient is attached to a provider within the organization — "attachment status elsewhere is not important." 02 simply flags primary care elsewhere.

3

OH's own template says "team-based"

The 2026-27 PCAP Implementation Plan asks for "the provider type(s) responsible for attachment (e.g., MD, NP, team-based)." OH opened the language door — while the counting stays MD/NP-anchored.

The non-negotiable

The one rule that protects everything

STATUS 01 = OPC + MD/NP (MRP) ASSIGNED

No exceptions. Enforced in the EMR: OPC cannot be saved without a populated MD/NP field.

We do not expand OPC

Widening OPC to include team-only relationships would corrupt Track A, contaminate every legitimate 01, and hand an auditor a systemic finding.

We flag Program_Attachment instead

Team-engaged patients carry Program_Attachment = Y plus Program_Type (multi-select). One patient record, one status, many program flags — duplication solved at the source.

Where every patient lands

The four statuses

01

Rostered to our MD / roster-taking NP

Counts toward the PCAP target. Includes Stage 2→3 conversions dated at actual rostering.

02

Primary care confirmed elsewhere

School-clinic children with family MDs; navigator patients attached to partners. Submitted; no target credit; proof of reach.

03

Program-engaged, no MD/NP anywhere

All interprofessional clients. MRP -99, dates 999999. Our conversion pipeline and system-reach evidence.

04

Genuinely unknown

Use sparingly. Time-boxed to two cycles. The trend must go down.

Submitting a large 03 volume is required and compliant — it is how our interprofessional footprint becomes visible to Ontario Health.

DSP Patients file

The attachment block: exact values

Attachment Status errors 410073–74

01 / 02 / 03 / 04 — two characters, leading zero ("01" not "1"). Only 01 scores.

Attachment Date errors 410077–79

YYYYMM of actual rostering. Attached but date unrecorded: first-contact date. Not attached: 999999. Never future-dated.

Unattached Date errors 410082–85

999999 while attached / never attached. De-roster or death: actual YYYYMM — must be after the Attachment Date.

MRP ID errors 410088–91

CPSO # (MD) or CNO registration (NP) for 01s; must reconcile with Staff-file MRP = Yes. All 02/03/04: -99.

Compliance

Bright lines

  • High 03 volumes — required, compliant, strategic
  • -99 and 999999 exactly as specified
  • First-contact date for attached patients with unrecorded roster dates (logged)
  • Counting arrivals from any route — no formal referral agreements needed
  • Penalty-free resubmission within the window
  • 01 without OPC + MD/NP — artificial rostering
  • Backdating, or program-entry date as attachment date
  • Expanding OPC criteria to team-only relationships
  • Allied health flagged or entered as MRP to lift 01s
  • Parking patients in 04 to avoid screening; letting 04 rise
The model

Dual-track reporting

Track A

OH / DSP compliance

  • Three CSVs, exactly to spec, monthly and quarterly
  • 01 count = PCAP target metric
  • 03 count = system reach, on OH's own portal
Track B

System attachment (internal)

  • Unique patients, HCN-deduped
  • Program_Attachment volume + engagement matrix
  • Tier distribution and navigation activity
The KPI that bridges both

Quarterly 03 → 01 conversion rate — the program pipeline becoming target attachment, on the record.
